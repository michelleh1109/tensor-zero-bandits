How TensorZero Solves Both Problems with Multi-Arm Bandits

Recap of traditional A/B methods:

Problem 1 Â· Fixed Sample Sizes

Problem 2 Â· Peeking leads to P-Hacking

TensorZero uses the Track-and-Stop (T&S) strategy to find the best-arm in the multi-arm bandit problem, i.e. finding the best lever to pull among many slot machines.

What makes "Track and Stop" unique?

1. Track: The algorithm calculates the optimal proportion of times to play each machine and tries to "track" (match) those proportions.

2. Stop: The "Stop" part means that once the algorithm is very confident it has found the best machine (based on statistical evidence), it stops exploring and focuses only on the best one.

"Track and Stop" Addresses P-Hacking

More on why p-hacking occurs:

You might be tempted to check your test statistic as experimentation data arrives and stop as soon as your data looks decent (typically p<0.05). However, this practice known as p-hacking or premature stopping unknowingly inflates your false positive rate i.e. choose the wrong version. This is known as â€œp-hackingâ€ or probability hacking.


1. Real Statistical Validity TensorZero addresses premature peeking, or p-hacking, using an anytime-valid statistical test. It allows you to check results and draw conclusions at any point without breaking statistical rules, ensuring your results are trustworhty and accurate.

2. Improving A/B Efficiency and Minimizing Losses In traditional experimentation, itâ€™s extremely unlikely that youâ€™d land on the optimal proportions for testing (i.e. the exact number of weeks to experiment for) by chance. TensorZero learns the optimal proportions (length of time, and share of traffic) as the experiment progresses over time, and dynamically allocates traffic to converge toward the best variants. In other words, we minimize the number of wrong arm pulls on the slot machine, analogous to minimizing the number of users who are shown a bad prompt. As soon as the track-and-stop algorithm has identified a winner with sufficient confidence, it stops the experiment and directs all subsequent traffic to that winner.

Configuring the Bandit

We typically provide sensible default parameters for most use cases that don't need editing, but you can customize the T&S algorithm for advanced TensorZero users. Here's the intuition:

1. Î´ (delta): Error tolerance Similar to a p-value. The probability the algorithm declares the wrong variant the winner. Default is 0.05 (5% error rate).

Contextualizing: For high-stakes experiments, billing flows, authentication, core conversion paths, you can set it lower like 0.01. For low-stakes tests like copy tweaks or formatting changes, 0.10 could be reasonable.

2. Îµ (epsilon): Minimum difference between variants At Îµ = 0, the algorithm won't stop until it's fully confident one variant is strictly the best. Setting Îµ > 0 means the algorithm will stop as soon as it finds any variant performing within Îµ of the best, which typically means a faster conclusion in exchange for occasionally missing a marginal win.

Example: Îµ = 0.05 and Variant A has a 72% win rate while Variant B has a 69% win rate, the algorithm treats them as equivalent and may declare either the winner, stopping sooner than if it had to prove A was strictly better.

Parameter Tradeoff

These parameters present a fundamental tradeoff: higher sensitivity and lower error rates require longer experiments. Decreasing Îµ enables detection of better arms but requires more samples to distinguish variants that are close in performance. Decreasing Î´ (e.g. from 0.05 to 0.01) provides stronger statistical guarantees but requires more samples since more evidence is needed to confidently identify the best variant.

Try it yourself

The quickstart gets you to a working experiment in under 15 minutes. Works with Claude, GPT-4, Gemini, or any OpenAI-compatible endpoint.

1

Define your JSON function & variants

Register extract_entities with an output schema, then declare your variants such as two different system prompt templates.

2

Add the experimentation block

Set type = "track_and_stop", list your candidate variants, name a metric, and set update_period_s to control how often allocations recalculate.

3

Define your metrics

Use exact_match (boolean) is the optimization metric. Add jaccard_similarity (float) is a tracked metric, not in the experimentation metric block.

4

Deploy

The gateway routes live traffic, shifts allocations every update_period_s, and stops the experiment once a winner is identified with statistical confidence.
